Let's try this to have a little better way to discuss our hot topics.
Title: "Q: your question", "C: your claim".

To keep things organised the format of each claim post body must be the following:
1. Claim - exactly 1 sentence. There's no limit to the number of posts, so if you have multiple concerns/points please create separate posts.
2. Evidence - must be in the post for everyone to assess. If the evidence is an email - please attach a screenshot, dates and a topic. If the evidence is a document, please name the document, where it was obtained (so we can e.g. know that honorarium was mentioned in the version sent over emails), page and a screenshot with the relevant section. Evidence should include enough surrounding context so screenshots are not misleading. This section is just the raw material, your commentary about it belongs in the next item. 
3. Commentary on the evidence above (and only on the evidence above). If there are multiple issues - please create multiple posts. If you feel some evidence is relevant for multiple points, please kindly include it in each post.
4. Requested action

Alternatively, if you only want to understand/clarify something for yourself and are seeking explanations and evidence, you may create a post with a question. In that case the format is the following:
1. What are you trying to understand? Please create multiple posts for multiple questions.
2. What document, decision, meeting, figure, or process are you asking about?
3. What would answer the question?

When you are writing your post (regardless of whether it's a claim or a question), please be specific and especially careful with the following things that we've seen frequently conflated:
1. current committee *vs* any previous committee. GOOD: "the committee before the last AGM", "the committee at the time of the new BM company selection", "current committee". BAD: "the committee"
2. special levy budget for the fire order project *vs* selected contractor quote *vs* selected contractor final contract sum. GOOD: "special levy budget", "contractor quote", "final contractor price". BAD: "20M project"
3. Fire order superintendent/fire order OC project manager *vs* fire order contractor's project manager. GOOD: "superintendent", "OC-side project manager", "contractor's project manager". BAD: "project manager"

Rules:
1. No references to things outside the post. This means no "you've seen it", "find and read it yourself" and anything like that. We all feel like we've said same things many times over, and it is frustrating, but it's not working. For guidance, think that a new owner should be able to read your post, read the evidence attached and have full information to both assess the claim based on the evidence, as well as be able to independently acquire that evidence.
2. No assumptions of and references to intent, motives, social relationships, and other unverifiable things. If you claim conflict of interest, please submit that as a post and attach evidence. We are frustrated with each other, it is natural to see the other side as unreasonable, but for this conversation to work we must keep that frustration to ourselves (myself included).
3. Posts that do not follow the format will be tagged as DRAFT and returned for editing (locked until fixed).

Reply/comment rules:
1. for Claim posts - focus on whether the evidence is sufficient to support the claim, and if it is, whether the requested action is appropriate for the claim.
2. for Question posts the reply should be either a plain explanation, ideally in the format similar to a claim post, or an actual claim post.
